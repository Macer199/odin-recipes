Carbonara

Description

A simple but iconic Italian classic, this dish is a delicious combination of pasta, eggs, cheese, and pork. Hailing from the Lazio region, this dish has taken its modern form and name in the middle of the 20th century. Whehter as an everyday dinner or special occasions, this dish is a luxurious meal that anyone can enjoy. The 30 minutes put into creating carbonara will be worth it.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of spaghetti
  • 1/2 pound of guanciale. (If you don't have guanciale, use pancetta. If you don't have pancetta, use bacon.)
  • 4 eggs
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • 1 cup of pecorino cheese

Steps

  1. Cut the guanciale into 1/2 inch cubes and place in a pan on medium heat.
  2. while the guanciale cooks, grate the pecorino cheese onto a plate.
  3. Boil water in a pot, and place a generous amount of salt in it. The salt gives the pasta flavor. Save some of the water.
  4. Place the spaghetti in the boiling water, and let it cook for 11 minutes.
  5. While the spaghetti cooks, crack the eggs and put 3 egg yolks and 1 whole egg in a bowl.
  6. Whisk the eggs with some pecorino cheese and black pepper.
  7. Once cooked and the fat rendered, put the guanciale into a bowl.
  8. When the pasta is cooked, place it onto the pan with the guanciale fat and mix and stop the heat. That way, the egg slurry we made will not cook the eggs. Stop the heat for the pot as well.
  9. Put the egg slurry and some guanciale on the pan and mix it with the spaghetti.
  10. Get a small cup of hot water from the pot, put in the pan, and mix well. The starch from the water will bind the sauce with the pasta.
  11. Serve on a plate and garnish with some of the guanciale and pecorino you may have left over.
  12. Enjoy!
